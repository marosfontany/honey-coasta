the artist background

Most of his life was spent in the capital or in his grandparent’s countryside house, shooting hoops and sunbathing in the backyard. ... In 4th grade, HoneyCoasta began playing the guitar, he attended a music school for some time as well, so for more than 10 years, he has been developing his skills as a guitar player. But his inner artist wouldn't let him stop at only playing the guitar.

HoneyCoasta over the years has taught himself how to play various percussion and acoustic instruments....Throughout the years, from 2017 to 2020, HoneyCoasta played in various bands and was a part of many projects. In late 2020 HoneyCoasta started studying music production at the University of West London.

meaning behind name

The stage name Honeycoasta is a multilayered description of what Honeycoasta is, what he does and what inspires him. “Honey” part of the name symbolises the artist’s love for nature and all things simple. Real is beautiful after all. “Coasta” part was born out of his misspelled birth name on a Starbucks cup. The word symbolises the latin music influence in his works as well as sunbathing under the Lithuanian sun.

inspirations

During the 3 years of playing in various bands, HoneyCoasta explored many genres like psychedelic rock, funk, progressive rock, indie & jazz-rock. However, his personal sound grew out of such genres as indie rock, bosa nova, and jazz. ... HoneyCoasta points out that the most influential artists to him are people like Tom Misch, Marcos Valle, and Oscar Jerome. Lyrically he is inspired by the perfect world of simplicity - nature. Reflected in his artworks, the beach, the Lithuanian fields & lakes, and the comfort of home are what inspire HoneyCoasta to write, play and create in general.
